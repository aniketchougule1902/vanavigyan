Curry leaves

Murraya koenigii

Culinary Herbs
Author: Yash Shinde
Published: 26/11/2025
Read Time: 5 min read
Category: Culinary Herbs
Location: Pune

Description

Curry leaves, also known as Kadi Patta or Kari Patta, are an aromatic, medicinal leaf widely used in everyday Indian cooking. The plant is a small to medium-sized shrub, and its leaves possess a strong fragrance.

In Ayurveda, curry leaves are specifically known for improving digestion, regulating blood sugar, boosting hair growth, and reducing inflammation in the body. The natural antioxidants present in the leaves (such as carbazole alkaloids) protect the body from toxins.




Medicinal Properties

antioxidant anti-inflammatory antidiabetic antimicrobial hepatoprotective (liver-protecting) anti-obesity digestive stimulant antihyperlipidemic

Common Uses

  • Used for tempering in Indian dishes
  • Chewing fresh leaves for digestion
  • Leaf paste applied for hair growth
  • Curry leaf powder mixed in food
  • Used in herbal decoctions ()
  • Curry leaf oil for scalp massage

Growing Information

Growing Conditions

Well-drained soil, partial to full sunlight, warm climate, regular pruning, organic compost rich soil

Water Requirements

Moderatewater 23 times per week; avoid waterlogging.

Temperature Range

20C 35C (Ideal tropical climate)

Harvesting Tips

Pluck mature dark green leaves, avoid over-plucking from young plants, harvest early morning for best aroma, prune branches to encourage bushy growth.

Preparation Methods

Curry Leaf Powder

Wash leaves Sun-dry fully Roast lightly Grind into fine powder Store airtight.

Curry Leaf Hair oil

Heat coconut oil Add fresh leaves Allow leaves to turn dark Cool Strain Store in bottle.

Curry leaf Paste

Crush fresh leaves with little water Use for scalp application or digestive remedies
