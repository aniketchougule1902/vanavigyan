Lemon grass

Cymbopogon citratus / Cymbopogon flexuosus

Aromatic Plants
Author: Yash Shinde
Published: 26/11/2025
Read Time: 5 min read
Category: Aromatic Plants
Location: Pune

Description

Lemongrass, also known as Nimbu Ghaas, is a tall, green, and aromatic grass with a natural lemon-like fragrance. In India, it is widely used in tea, herbal decoctions, soups, and traditional medicines. Its leaves contain powerful essential oils, especially citral, which helps reduce inflammation, improves digestion, and calms the mind.


In Ayurveda, lemongrass is used for:


Stomach pain, gas, indigestion


Cough, cold, and fever


Controlling blood pressure


Reducing stress


Detoxification



Because of its strong antibacterial and antifungal properties, it also helps the body fight infections.

Growing Information

Growing Conditions

Well-drained soil, full sunlight, warm climate, regular pruning, organic compost, grows well in pots, avoid water stagnation

Water Requirements

Moderatewater every 23 days; keep soil moist but not waterlogged.

Temperature Range

20C 35C (thrives in warm tropical climate)

Harvesting Tips

Cut stalks from base when 12 cm thick, harvest early morning for best aroma, avoid over-harvesting from one clump, dry leaves for storage, trim regularly to promote fresh growth.

Preparation Methods

Lemongrass Tea

Boil water Add chopped leaves Boil 5 minutes Strain Drink for digestion & cold.

Lemongrass Paste

Crush fresh stalks Make paste Apply on swollen or painful area.

Lemongrass Powder

Wash leaves Shade dry Grind Store Use in tea or detox drinks.
