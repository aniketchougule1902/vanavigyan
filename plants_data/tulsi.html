Tulsi

Ocimum Tenuiflorum

Medicinal Plants
Author: Carl Linnaeus
Published: 5/1/1753
Read Time: 5 min
Category: Medicinal Plants
Location: Pune, Maharashtra, India

Description

Tulsi is known as “Mother Tulsi” and “Holy Basil” in India. It is a small bush which grows 30-60 cm tall, has fragrant leaves and small purple or pink flowers. It is planted in the courtyard of every house because it is considered an antidote to sin and good for health.

Medicinal Properties

Adaptogenic Immunomodulator Anti-inflammatory Antibacterial Antiviral Antioxidant Antifungal Antidiabetic Antistress Expectorant Analgesic Antipyretic Cardioprotective Radioprotective

Common Uses

  • Fresh 4–5 leaves chewed daily on empty stomach
  • Tulsi kadha (decoction) for cold, cough & fever
  • Tulsi tea for stress relief and immunity
  • Tulsi ark (distilled extract) – 10-15 drops in water
  • Tulsi powder with honey for respiratory problems
  • Tulsi juice for skin problems and acne
  • Tulsi smoke (dhoop) for purifying air and mosquito repellent

Growing Information

🌱 Growing Conditions

Full sunlight (6–8 hours daily) Well-drained loamy or sandy-loam soil pH 6–7.5 Can be grown in pots, balconies, terrace Seeds or stem cuttings for propagation Perennial plant (lives 2–3 years easily)

💧 Water Requirements

Moderate; water when top soil feels dry (usually every 2–3 days in summer, less in winter). Avoid waterlogging.

🌡️ Temperature Range

Ideal: 20°C – 35°C Can tolerate up to 40°C in summer Minimum tolerable: 10°C (protect in winter below 15°C)

✂️ Harvesting Tips

Harvest leaves in the morning after dew dries Pluck top 3–4 leaves with stem to encourage bushy growth Never remove more than 30% of plant at once Regular plucking increases lifespan and yield

Preparation Methods

Fresh leaf juice

10–20 leaves crushed and strained

Kadha

15–20 fresh leaves boiled in 2 cups water till 1 cup remains

Tulsi tea

5–7 fresh or 1 tsp dried leaves steeped in hot water 5–10 min

Powder

Shade-dry leaves and grind, store in air-tight jar
