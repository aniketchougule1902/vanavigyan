Turmeric

Curcuma longa

Medicinal Plants
Author: Linnaeus (L.)
Published: 5/1/1753
Read Time: 7 min
Category: Medicinal Plants
Location: Pune, Maharashtra, India

Description

Turmeric is a perennial herb that belongs to the ginger family. Its root (rhizome) is dried to form the yellow-orange powder we call turmeric. In Ayurveda, it's called "Haridra" and has been used for over 4,000 years.

Medicinal Properties

Anti-inflammatory Antioxidant Antiseptic Antibacterial Antiviral Anti-cancer Analgesic (pain-reliever) Hepatoprotective (liver protector) Digestive Blood purifier Wound healer Immunomodulator

Common Uses

  • Drinking golden milk daily to boost immunity
  • Applying fresh turmeric paste on cuts, burns, or wounds for quick healing
  • Taking turmeric with warm milk during sprains, injuries, or body pain
  • Using turmeric face pack (with curd, besan, or milk) for acne, pigmentation, and glowing skin
  • Consuming turmeric regularly for joint pain and arthritis relief
