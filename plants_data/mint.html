Mint

Mentha arvensis

Culinary Herbs
Author: Yash Shinde
Published: 26/11/2025
Read Time: 5 min read
Category: Culinary Herbs
Location: Pune

Description

Mint, commonly known as "Pudina" in everyday language, is an extremely aromatic and cooling-natured plant. Its leaves are green, soft, and provide an immediate sensation of coolness when placed in the mouth. It is easy to grow at home and is used in Ayurveda to improve digestion, reduce gas and acidity, calm headaches, and provide relief from skin irritation.

Mint contains natural menthol, which provides instant cooling to the body and mind. It soothes the stomach, eliminates bad breath, and offers relief from vomiting and nausea.

Major Medicinal Uses:

  1. Gas, Indigestion, Vomiting
  2. Headache, Sinus & Nasal Blockage
  3. Skin Itching and Irritation
  4. Bad Breath (Halitosis)
  5. Stress Relief (via aromatic therapy)

Key Citations:

Growing Information

Growing Conditions

Moist fertile soil, partial sunlight, frequent pruning, shallow container-friendly, fast-growing runners, organic manure recommended

Water Requirements

Highsoil should remain consistently moist; water every 12 days in summer.

Temperature Range

15C 30C (thrives in cool to warm climate)

Harvesting Tips

Harvest early morning for best aroma, pluck top leaves to encourage bushy growth, avoid flowering by pruning regularly, never remove more than one-third of plant at a time.

Preparation Methods

Mint tea

Boil water Add fresh leaves Steep 5 minutes Drink warm for digestion & cold relief.

Mint Paste

Crush leaves with little water Apply on skin for cooling, itching or rashes.

Mint Powder

Wash leaves Shade dry Grind into fine powder Store airtight Add in teas or detox drinks.

Mint steam

Boil water Add mint leaves Inhale steam for blocked nose & headache
