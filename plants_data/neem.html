Neem

Azadirachta indica A.Juss

Medicinal Plants
Author: H. S. Puri
Published: 11/23/1999
Read Time: 6 Min
Category: Medicinal Plants
Location: Pune, Maharashtra, India

Description

Neem tree is India's oldest and most effective medicinal tree. It is called "village pharmacy" because everything on it - leaves, bark, seeds, oil - works as medicine. Neem purifies the blood, controls sugar levels, cures all skin diseases (pimples, rashes, itching, eczema), strengthens teeth and heals the mouth. It is anti-bacterial, anti-viral, anti-fungal, hence fights infections. It is also beneficial in fevers like malaria and dengue. It also keeps the liver and kidneys clean. It has been used since ancient times to purify blood, kill stomach worms and remove heat.

Medicinal Properties

Antibacterial Antiviral Antifungal Antiseptic Anti-inflammatory Blood purifier Immuno-modulator Antidiabetic Antimalarial Antiparasitic Wound healing Antioxidant Analgesic Antipyretic

Common Uses

  • Skin diseases (acne, eczema, psoriasis)
  • Diabetes control
  • Dental problems (toothache, bleeding gums, mouth ulcers)
  • Blood purification
  • Malaria & dengue fever
  • Worm infestation in children
  • Dandruff & hair fall
  • Wound healing
  • Contraceptive (seed oil – traditional)
  • Pesticide (natural insect repellent)

Growing Information

🌱 Growing Conditions

Full sunlight (6–8 hours daily), Well-drained sandy/loamy soil, pH 6.2–7.5, Tolerates drought & poor soil, Grows fast in hot climate, Can be grown in big pots or ground, Space 15–20 feet apart if in ground

💧 Water Requirements

Low to moderate – water only when top soil is dry (once in 7–10 days in summer, less in monsoon & winter). Very drought tolerant after 1 year.

🌡️ Temperature Range

Ideal: 25–40°C, Can survive up to 48°C and down to 0–5°C (young plants need protection below 10°C)

✂️ Harvesting Tips

Leaves: Pick young tender leaves anytime, best before flowering (Feb–May) Seeds: Collect ripe yellow fruits from tree in May–June, dry in shade Bark: Harvest only from mature trees (>5 years), peel gently in summer Never strip whole tree – take only 20–30%

Preparation Methods

Neem leaf powder

Dry leaves in shade → grind → sieve

Neem water (for skin/blood purify)

Boil 20–30 fresh leaves in 1 liter water → cool → drink or wash

Neem oil

From seeds (coldily pressed) – for skin & hair

Neem paste

Fresh leaves + little water → grind → apply on skin/wounds
