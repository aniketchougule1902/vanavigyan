Ajwain

Trachyspermum ammi

Medicinal Plants
Author: Yash Shinde
Published: 26/11/2025
Read Time: 5 min Read
Category: Medicinal Plants
Location: Pune

Description

Ajwain is a small aromatic plant whose seeds are widely used in Indian homes as a natural medicine. The seeds have a strong smell and sharp taste. In Ayurveda, Ajwain is mainly known for improving digestion, reducing gas, and relieving stomach cramps. It is also helpful in cold, cough, sore throat, toothache, joint pain, and infections because of its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.


Ajwain oil is extremely strong and widely used for chest congestion and joint massage. The plant grows easily at home and needs warm sunlight.

(Key citations: Ayurvedic texts & ethnobotanical studies mention its strong digestive, antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities.)

Medicinal Properties

antiseptic anti-inflammatory antispasmodic analgesic (pain-relieving) antimicrobial antifungal carminative (gas-relieving) expectorant antioxidant

Common Uses

  • Ajwain water for digestion and gas
  • Seeds chewed for stomach cramps
  • Ajwain steam inhalation for cold and blocked nose
  • Ajwain oil for chest congestion
  • Ajwain paste for toothache
  • Ajwain smoke for curing damp smell and insects
  • Ajwain seed tea for menstrual pain
  • Ajwain powder for acidity

Growing Information

Growing Conditions

well-drained soil, full sunlight, warm climate, regular pruning, moderate organic manure, avoid waterlogging

Water Requirements

Light to moderate watering; water only when top soil becomes dry.

Temperature Range

20C to 35C (ideal warm climate)

Harvesting Tips

Harvest seeds when flower heads turn pale brown Cut the whole cluster and dry it in shade Separate seeds by rubbing gently Store in airtight container to preserve aroma

Preparation Methods

Ajwain Water

Boil 1 tsp seeds in water; filter and drink warm.

Ajwain Powder

Dry-roast seeds lightly and grind.

Ajwain Oil

Extracted through steam distillation (home use: warm seeds in mustard oil).

Ajwain Kadha

Boil seeds with tulsi + ginger for cold & cough.

Chemical Compounds

thymol gamma-terpinene p-cymene beta-pinene carvacrol flavonoids tannins saponins
