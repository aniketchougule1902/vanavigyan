Cloves

Syzygium aromaticum

Medicinal Plants
Author: Yash Shinde
Published: 26/11/2025
Read Time: 5 min read
Category: Medicinal Plants
Location: Pune

Description

Cloves, known in Hindi as Laung, are aromatic flower buds that are dried and used as a spice. They have a strong, warm, and slightly pungent flavor. Cloves contain a very potent medicinal oil, especially eugenol, which helps reduce pain, kills harmful bacteria, and improves digestion.


In Ayurveda, cloves are used for:


Toothache and gum inflammation


Cold, cough, and sore throat


Improving digestion, reducing gas and nausea


Headache


Preventing infections


Removing bad breath



Clove oil provides quick relief from pain and swelling because of its strong analgesic and antiseptic properties.

Growing Information

Growing Conditions

Tropical climate, deep fertile soil, partial shade to full sun, good drainage, high humidity, organic mulch, sheltered from strong winds (Note: Clove is a tree; not ideal for small homes but can grow in large open spaces.)

Water Requirements

Highrequires moist soil; water regularly, avoid drying.

Temperature Range

20C 30C (cannot tolerate frost)

Harvesting Tips

Harvest flower buds before blooming, pick when buds turn green to light pink, sun-dry until they turn brown, store airtight, avoid harvesting from very young trees.

Preparation Methods

Clove Tea

Boil water Add 23 cloves Simmer 5 minutes Drink for cold, cough & digestion.

Clove oil

Warm coconut oil Add crushed cloves Heat lightly Cool Strain Apply on toothache or gums.

Clove steam

Add cloves to hot water Inhale for cough and nasal blockage.

Clove Paste

Grind cloves with warm water Apply gently on gums for pain relief.
